Georgian National Anthem

The Georgian National Anthem is called "Tavisupleba", which means "Freedom." The music was written by Zakaria Paliashvili, and the words were written by Davit Maghradze. It was adopted in 2004, after the Rose Revolution in Georgia. The anthem shows the pride and strength of the Georgian people. It talks about their desire for freedom, peace, and unity. The music is strong and inspiring, reflecting the spirit of Georgia and its culture.
